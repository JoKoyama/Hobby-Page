Padlocks

A padlock is one of the more common locks due to it's small size and wide compatibility. They can be used on lockers, cabinets, and/or on backpacks. This also means padlocks are most often lost or the combination cannot be recalled, resulting in a higher rate of loss due to the relatively light material they're made from. Most of these locks can be found from the makers of; Master Lock, Brinks, and Medeco

Key Padlocks


Key padlocks use a cylinder which require a key to open the lock. These cylinders have springs with varying strengths which pushes the driver pins and key pins. These space between the 2 pins must reach the shear line in order for the key to turn. How this occurs is by the key pins resting on the cuts of the key which also varies in height.
The more cuts you have in a Key, the more durable it is. This is due to the increase in pins a person must lift to open a lock if they are picking the lock. Another strong point of this lock is that you can make more keys at a nearby lock smith or key manufacturer. The down sides of these locks are that if you have fewer than 5~4 cuts in the key thy could be opened by a bump key or easily picked, as 5 to 4 cuts in a key are some of the more common number of cuts. Also if you lose the key to the lock you cannot open the lock without using brute force.

Combination Padlocks

A combination padlock is the most widely used padlock due to its changeable combinations or the need for no keys. These padlocks use a wheel thats is stacked on top of each other. These wheels have a groove or indent on the wheel which when matched allow the pins on the bar or the lever to pass though, resulting the openning of the lock. A big difference from the key padlocks is that these locks use probability rather than the height difference. The reason most Key padlocks are easy to pick is due to the fact that you can match all the pins on complete accident if they are lifted in a certain way (bump key method), whereas combination padlocks which the correct combination is the same as any other, so it is hard to distinguish the correct combination from the wrong ones. The use of no keys makes the lock one object rather than 2 of key and lock. Forgetting these combonations may be one of the fatal weaknesses as you have to guess from 100 to around 64,000 combonations for consumer locks. Although there are more specific types of locks most of those locks will fit into these 2 categories.
